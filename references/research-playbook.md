# Research Playbook

Use this guide before drafting the article.

## Goal

Do not start from a blank page if the topic already has good published examples.

Search for:

- primary sources that provide facts
- current reporting or recent posts that update the facts
- exemplar articles that already proved the topic can work as content

Use Tavily as the default research search layer when it is configured.
Record the exact search queries you used and keep the returned source list inside `research.sources`.

## Exemplar Standard

Collect at least 1 exemplar article in `research.exemplars`.

Prefer articles that show one or more of these signals:

- visible likes, shares, views, reads, or favorites
- search snippets that mention `10w+`, `高赞`, `爆文`, `热门`, `viral`, or similar wording
- credible longform platforms with strong distribution
- a structure or angle worth borrowing even if exact engagement numbers are not visible

If exact engagement numbers are unavailable, record that plainly in `engagementEvidence`.

## How To Use Exemplars

Do not paraphrase or clone them.

Use exemplars to decide:

- whether the topic already has reader demand
- what angle readers respond to
- how the headline frames tension
- how the article sequences facts, stakes, and takeaways

Then write a new article with its own judgment, evidence, and wording.

## Minimum Record

For each exemplar, capture:

- `title`
- `url`
- `platform`
- `engagementEvidence`
- `reason`
- `takeaways`

`reason` should explain what to borrow.
`takeaways` should list the useful structural or editorial lessons.
